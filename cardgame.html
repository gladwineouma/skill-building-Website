A game full of fun and learning, by the end of the game you will have learnt more about natural selection. Natural selection is simply the nature through which living organisms adapt and change in response to an environmental condtion.

HOW NATURAL SELECTION IS PLAYED

This is how to play the game: Firstly, place the game board in the center. Shuffle the animal cards and create a draw pile. Each player draws 4 cards to form their initials at hand. Players keep cards hidden from their opponents. Players take turns in a specific order, On ones turn they play a card that either has the matching color or number of the previously played and if they do not have that card they must drop one to the pile and wait for the next round. We have action cards in the game and their role is to give the next player a challenge to do. The action cards are dynamic elements to the game.

There are also other cards that give different tasks like: Skip card, which gets the next player to be skipped, Reverse card, which changes the current direction of the game, Wild card, which allows players to choose any color they want and continue while reflecting adaptability, Drop two cards, which forces the player to drop any two cards and the Swap card, which allows the players to swap hands akin to species forming a symbolic relationship. To win the game, you must ensure to have cards at all times. Therefore the winner it the player with the most remaining cards, this will symbolize successful adaptation and survival of that player in this particular environment hence natural selection.

Card matching: Players must match the color or number of the top card on the discard pile.

Action Card: Action cards can be played at any time, regardless of matching colors or numbers.

End of turn: After playing or drawing, the players turn ends and play passes to the next player.

Elimination: Higher-numbered cards can eliminate lower-numbered cards in play, allowing for strategic challenges against opponents.

Environmental Adaptation: Players should adapt their strategies based on the cards played and changing game dynamics.
